What to Order Anywhere
When You're Watching Calories

Exact ordering scripts. Real places. No judgment.

These are restaurant swaps, not a diet plan — exact orders for when you're eating out and don't want to lose ground. Written by Laini Byfield, a National Board Certified Health and Wellness Coach (NBC-HWC) and behavioral eating pattern specialist.

Fast Food

McDonald's
Order thisDouble burger — no cheese, no sauce, no ketchup. Toss the top bun. Mustard optional.
Wendy's
Order thisGrilled chicken salad. No dressing. Same grilled chicken as the sandwich — confirmed at the counter.
Watch out: Dressing erases the math faster than anything else on this menu.
Chick-fil-A
BreakfastChicken burrito — no cheese, no hash browns, jalapeño salsa for flavor. Ask for grilled chicken instead of fried. The tortilla makes it work; a bowl feels like diet food and you'll resent it. Don't beat yourself up if they forget the grilled swap — it happens.
Lunch / DinnerGrilled nuggets, no sauce. Market salad, no dressing, no granola.
Watch out: The sauces turn this into a different meal. Plain is the order.
Culver's
Order thisBurger, no bun. Broccoli side.
O'Charley's
Order thisPlain wings. Dry. No sauce.

Fast Casual

Chipotle
Order thisBowl — no chips, no tortilla. Any protein; chicken has the most. Lettuce, fajita veggies, black beans, salsa. Add guac if your energy feels low.
Jimmy John's
Order thisAny sandwich as an Unwich. Double meat. Skip mayo. Avocado, mustard, cucumbers, tomatoes, pickles, oregano as wanted.
Worth the add: Double meat and avocado keep you from needing to eat again in two hours.
Mexican — Any Location
Order thisChicken fajita. One tortilla. Salsa and pico on the side. Skip cheese, sour cream, rice. Fork and knife for the rest.
Chinese — Any Location
Order thisMoo shu chicken or beef. One wrapper. Fork and knife for the rest.
Watch out: Hoisin sauce is high sugar. Use lightly or skip.

Sit-Down Chains

Olive Garden
Order thisMinestrone first — not breadsticks. Simplest chicken entrée. Dressing on the side, don't use it. Cut your chicken into the salad. The salad is the meal.
Watch out: Breadsticks arrive automatically. Order the minestrone immediately so you have something to do with your hands.
Chili's / TGI Fridays / Applebee's
StrategyFind the simplest preparation. Ask: "Is this grilled to order or reheated?" Request baked if possible. Plain side salad or steamed vegetables.
Watch out: See grilled chicken warning above. Applies here more than anywhere.
Pizza

Damage control, not optimization. Both are valid skills.

StrategyShishito peppers or side salad first. Fill up on diet soda while others eat bread. Thin crust — toppings, leave the crust. Thick crust — cheese and toppings only.

Coffee Stops

Starbucks
DrinkTall iced latte, sugar-free vanilla, sugar-free protein cold foam. Sip slowly — it's closer to a protein smoothie than coffee. Sugar-free vanilla is the only consistent sugar-free syrup across locations.
FoodEgg and apple protein box. Skip grapes. Skip the egg yolk. Skip the Orzo round unless lightheaded. Egg bites as supplement only — skip cheese if pairing with the box.
Watch out: Egg bites look high-protein. They're not. More carbs than they appear. The box is the anchor.

Airport Survival

Hudson News / Convenience Stores
Look forHard boiled eggs. String cheese. Beef or turkey jerky — check sugar on the label. RXBARs or bars with 12g+ protein. Not exciting. Functional.
Subway
Order thisProtein bowl — no bread. Double meat. Any vegetables. Mustard or oil and vinegar. Not on every menu board but any location can make it.
Jamba Juice
Order thisPassion fruit if available. Any green smoothie with protein add. Skip peanut butter chocolate.
Watch out: Ask what the base is. Some use juice — sugar water. Ask for water base.

The Supermarket Move

Rotisserie Chicken + Broccoli Slaw

Works at any grocery with a hot food section. Costs less than fast food. Two to four servings.

What you need: Rotisserie chicken. Bag of broccoli slaw. One citrus — lemon, lime, or orange. Salt, pepper, hot sauce.

What you do: Pour slaw into the rotisserie container lid. Squeeze citrus over it. Season. Flake breast meat onto the slaw. Eat as a salad. You won't finish it.

The bowl problem: Publix switched to paper containers. Keep a Fozzils Snapfold Bowl XL in your bag. Collapses flat, rinses clean.

No affiliation. Just the bowl that works.

Bar & Social

Not abstinence. Drinks that don't undo the work.

Bloody Mary

Tomato base, low sugar, savory. One of the better cocktail choices calorically.

Rum and Diet

Diet mixer keeps the sugar out. Well rum is fine here.

Wine Spritzer

White wine with sparkling water. Lower ABV, lower calories, longer drink.

On the Rocks

Spirits on ice. Nothing added means nothing extra.

Hard Seltzer

Most predictable at standing events. Low sugar, easy to nurse.

Dirty Martini

Olive brine, no sugar. Slow drink — works in your favor.

Watch out: Decide your number before the first drink arrives. Not after.

Networking & Catered Events

No menu. No order moment. Set the strategy before you walk in.

Four appetizers is dinner. Whether it's a good dinner is up to you. Decide your number at the door.
Show up mostly full. You're there to network. The food is incidental.
On Arrival
First moveGet your drink. Walk the full table before taking anything. Find the protein anchor — meat, grilled chicken, anything without pastry or sauce. One plate. Step away from the table before eating.
The Triage Hierarchy

When there are no clean options, choose from the top of what's available.

Best to leastProtein + fat → fat + minimal sugar → sugar + some fat → pure carb.
A salmon cake is a concession, not a recommendation. Still better than cheesecake. Cheesecake is still better than an apple turnover.
One Plate Rule
The resetTake one plate. Step away. Finish it. Ask whether you're actually still hungry before going back. The second pass is almost never hunger — it's a lull in conversation.

Hot Tips

The social stigma of dieting is real. Nobody wants to explain themselves at a dinner table or a work event. These are the moves that keep you on plan without making it anyone else's business.

🌮 Wrapper Rule: Mexican
The move Order the fajita. Use one tortilla. Eat the rest with fork and knife. Nobody asks questions and nobody notices.
🥟 Wrapper Rule: Chinese
The move Order moo shu chicken or beef. One wrapper. Vegetable volume fills faster than plain protein. Fork and knife for the rest.
📦 The Take Home Box Move
The move When the food arrives, put the carbs you're not eating in a to-go box immediately. Tell the table "half now, half for later." Later means the dogs, the kids, the trash compactor, or the garbage. Nobody tracks what happens after the meal. What matters is that it's no longer in front of you — because food sitting on the table is a willpower problem, not a discipline problem.

Two things that are never what they appear

Grilled chicken at chains

Pre-cooked, shipped frozen, reheated, finished in butter. Ask for baked. Assume it was still touched by oil.

Restaurant rice

Finished in oil, seasoned with salt and sugar. That's why it tastes so good. Order around it.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the lowest calorie order at Chipotle?

Bowl with chicken, lettuce, fajita veggies, black beans, and salsa. Skip the tortilla, chips, cheese, and sour cream. Chicken has the highest protein. Add guac only if your energy feels low.

What should I order at McDonald's on a calorie restriction?

Double burger, no cheese, no sauce, no ketchup. Remove the top bun. Mustard optional. The salad is no longer on the menu.

What can I drink at a bar when I'm watching calories?

Hard seltzer is the most predictable at standing events. Otherwise: spirits on the rocks, dirty martini, rum and diet, wine spritzer, or a bloody mary. Decide your number before the first drink arrives.

How do I eat at a networking event without overeating?

Show up mostly full. Get your drink first. Walk the full food table before taking anything. Take one plate, step away before eating. Four appetizers is dinner — decide your number at the door.

What is the best low calorie order at Starbucks?

Tall iced latte, sugar-free vanilla, sugar-free protein cold foam. For food: egg and apple protein box — skip the grapes, skip the egg yolk, skip the Orzo round unless lightheaded.

What should I eat at the airport on a calorie restriction?

Hudson News convenience stores: hard boiled eggs, string cheese, beef or turkey jerky, RXBARs with 12g+ protein. At Subway: protein bowl, no bread, double meat. At Jamba Juice: green smoothie with protein add, water base.
